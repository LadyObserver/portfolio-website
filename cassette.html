OBJECTIVE

'80s music player app

According to the Design Brief, the objective of this project was to design the UI for an '80s music player mobile app that is targeted primarily at an audience between 35 and 55 years old.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Loneliness epidemic

As our world is more and more affected by the use of digital tools, we are becoming lonelier year by year. In fact, this loneliness epidemic can cause more harm than smoking a pack of cigarettes a day does to your body. Furthermore, surveys say that people over 30 tend to become isolated more easily since making friends gets harder as we age.

What can we do about this? We must create environments - even digital ones - where adult relationships can grow organically.

SOLUTION

Being in synch with others

The Cassette mobile app offers a solution for the chronic loneliness by offering tools that produce a massive hit of endorphins. As we sing and dance to the best '80s tunes with others in synch, we start bonding with them deeply. By using Cassette, it is guaranteed that you are going to find a concert buddy; going solo will be all in the past.

Cassette has the following key features:

  • Searching for content
  • Listening to content offline
  • Creating playlists
  • Streaming lyrics to smart Tvs
  • Group sessions
  • Discussion boards
  • Marketplace of '80s relics

CONCLUSION

Challenges

Since Cassette was my first UI project, I had to go back to polish the design and add some animations a couple of months later. I noticed that I had not followed the proper rules for naming the documents at that time so I had some difficulties to find them.

Next steps

In the next iteration I would focus on creating the discussion boards. This feature would emphasize that this app is actually more about making connections with others, and music is mainly the chemical bond that creates this connection between the app's users.
